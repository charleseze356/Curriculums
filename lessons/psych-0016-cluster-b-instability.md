*This lesson zooms into Cluster B from the personality map. Place dramatic behavior by what it is trying to secure or ignore: attachment, attention, admiration, or others’ rights.*

## Four functions of “wild” behavior

| Disorder | Dominant pattern | Clinical anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Borderline | Prevent abandonment amid unstable self/relationships | Splitting, impulsivity, self-harm; DBT |
| Histrionic | Secure attention | Theatrical, emotionally expressive, provocative |
| Narcissistic | Secure admiration/status | Grandiosity, entitlement, low empathy |
| Antisocial | Pursue goals despite others' rights | Deceit, aggression, irresponsibility, lack of remorse; conduct history |

*Sources: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry p. 403; FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-3, pp. 779–781.*

## The management relationship

Borderline personality disorder is the load-bearing Cluster B diagnosis because it couples relationship instability to acute safety. Assess suicidality and self-harm directly. Keep team boundaries clear and consistent to reduce splitting; use DBT as the primary psychotherapy, with medication only as an adjunct for target symptoms.
