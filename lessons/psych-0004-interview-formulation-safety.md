*This lesson zooms into the clerkship-performance layer from the map. Place it after diagnostic triage because the interview is where triage, diagnosis, workup, treatment, and safety become one clinical loop.*

## The loop

The syllabus requires Charles to independently conduct an interview, assess self-harm risk and acuity, and discuss a case formulation including diagnosis, workup, treatment planning, and safety considerations.

That gives the core loop:

1. **Elicit symptoms and timeline.**
2. **Locate source:** primary psychiatric vs substance vs medical.
3. **Build differential using overlap and duration.**
4. **Assess acute risk:** self-harm, harm to others, inability to care for self, agitation, psychosis, intoxication/withdrawal.
5. **Choose workup and level of care.**
6. **Choose treatment and safety plan.**

*Sources: Psychiatry Clerkship Syllabus objectives #3–6; FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry Ch. 27-1 and 27-17.*

---

## Why safety sits inside formulation

In psychiatry, diagnosis and disposition are tightly coupled. A patient with depression and no acute safety risk may be treated outpatient. A patient with depression, psychosis, hopelessness, and passive suicidality may require hospitalization.

So the formulation is not complete until it answers:

- What is most likely?
- What cannot be missed?
- What is the acuity?
- What level of care is safe?
