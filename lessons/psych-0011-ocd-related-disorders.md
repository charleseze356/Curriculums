*This lesson zooms into the obsession-relief loop from the anxiety map. Place the symptom by asking whether the patient is distressed by intrusive thoughts, compelled to neutralize them, or instead values rigid perfectionism.*

## OCD's mechanism

Obsessions are recurrent intrusive thoughts, images, or impulses that increase distress. Compulsions are repetitive behaviors or mental acts performed to reduce that distress. The temporary relief teaches the loop to repeat.

*Sources: FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-10, pp. 794–796; FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry p. 395.*

## The high-yield distinction

| Feature | OCD | OCPD |
|---|---|---|
| Core problem | Obsessions and/or compulsions | Pervasive perfectionism, order, and control |
| Experience | Ego-dystonic; unwanted and distressing | Ego-syntonic; experienced as appropriate |
| Behavior | Ritual reduces obsession-driven anxiety | Rigidity impairs efficiency and relationships |
| Treatment anchor | ERP + SSRI; clomipramine alternative | Psychotherapy |

## Related phenotypes

FA's algorithm places body dysmorphic disorder, hoarding disorder, trichotillomania, and excoriation disorder beside OCD. The unifying relationship is repetitive preoccupation or behavior, but the clinical phenotype—not the generic word “compulsion”—determines the diagnosis.
