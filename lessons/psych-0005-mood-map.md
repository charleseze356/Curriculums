*This is the map lesson for mood disorders. Every subsequent mood lesson hangs off this framework: abnormal mood → rule out source → polarity → psychosis/safety → treatment.*

## The mood algorithm

FA organizes abnormal mood by first asking whether a substance/medication or medical condition is inducing the mood disorder. Only after that does the map branch into MDD, bipolar I, bipolar II, grief, adjustment disorder, persistent depressive disorder, and cyclothymia.

*Source: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry Ch. 27-7, Fig. 27.7.*

---

## The core branches

| Branch | Clinical anchor |
|---|---|
| MDD | Major depressive episode: depressed mood/anhedonia plus SIGECAPS pattern for ≥2 weeks |
| MDD with psychotic features | Depression plus psychosis; consider antipsychotics and hospitalization |
| Bipolar I | At least one manic episode |
| Bipolar II | Hypomania plus depressive episode |
| Persistent depressive disorder | Chronic depressive symptoms ≥2 years |
| Cyclothymia | Chronic hypomanic/depressive fluctuations that do not meet full criteria |
| Adjustment disorder | Mood/anxiety/conduct symptoms within 3 months of stressor, resolving within 6 months after stressor ends |

---

## Why mood comes before psychosis

Psychosis can sit inside mood disease. If hallucinations or delusions occur only during mood episodes, FA places that under mood disorder with psychotic features. If psychosis persists for at least 2 weeks without mood symptoms, schizoaffective disorder becomes possible.

That distinction depends on already understanding mood episodes.
