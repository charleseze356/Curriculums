*This lesson zooms into bipolar polarity from the mood map. Place it after MDD because many bipolar patients present depressed first, and missing bipolarity changes treatment risk.*

## Bipolar I vs bipolar II

| Diagnosis | Required pattern |
|---|---|
| Bipolar I | At least one manic episode |
| Bipolar II | At least one hypomanic episode plus at least one depressive episode |

Mania and hypomania share DIGFAST symptoms, but differ by duration and consequence.

*Source: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry Ch. 27-7 and 27-9.*

---

## Mania vs hypomania

| Feature | Mania | Hypomania |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | ≥7 days | ≥4 days |
| Psychosis | Can occur | No psychotic features |
| Hospitalization/impairment | Present or may be required | Not present |
| Treatment implication | Often urgent mood stabilizer/antipsychotic ± hospitalization | Mood stabilizer-based plan, protect against manic switch |

---

## Acute mania management logic

FA's bipolar mania algorithm asks:

- Does the patient meet criteria for mania?
- Are they on antidepressants? → taper/stop.
- Is there severe agitation? → benzodiazepine ± antipsychotic.
- Is there psychosis? → add antipsychotic.
- Is there renal disease? → favor valproate over lithium.
- Is the case refractory? → switch/add antipsychotic or consider ECT.

---

## Mood stabilizer anchors

| Agent | High-yield role/risk |
|---|---|
| Lithium | First-line acute mania/maintenance; reduces suicide risk; renal, thyroid, calcium, toxicity, pregnancy risks |
| Valproate | First-line mood stabilizer; useful with renal disease; avoid liver disease, thrombocytopenia, pregnancy |
| Atypical antipsychotics | Acute mania and maintenance roles; monitor metabolic/EPS/QT/prolactin risks depending agent |
| Lamotrigine | Maintenance role; slow titration for Stevens-Johnson risk |
| ECT | Refractory mania, severe suicidality, psychotic depression, catatonic schizophrenia; generally safe in pregnancy |

The exam move is not "name every drug." It is choosing the safest drug for the patient's constraint.
