*This lesson zooms into Cluster C from the personality map. Place the pattern by how anxiety is managed: avoiding rejection, attaching to a caretaker, or controlling details and rules.*

## Three anxiety-management strategies

| Disorder | Strategy | Key contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Avoidant | Withdraw to prevent criticism/rejection | Wants relationships; unlike schizoid PD |
| Dependent | Submit and cling to preserve care | Difficulty deciding or acting independently |
| OCPD | Control details, order, morals, and performance | Ego-syntonic; unlike ego-dystonic OCD |

*Sources: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry p. 403; FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-3, pp. 780–781.*

## Treatment and interaction

Psychotherapy is the backbone. Avoid power struggles: offer clear recommendations while preserving patient agency. Medication treats comorbid anxiety or mood symptoms, not the enduring personality pattern itself.

## Week 2 integration

Anxiety disorders and Cluster C can look similar because both involve fear. The decisive question is whether symptoms form an episodic syndrome experienced as distressing, or an enduring ego-syntonic interpersonal style that spans contexts.
