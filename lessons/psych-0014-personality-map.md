*This is the map lesson for personality disorders. Every subsequent lesson in this chapter hangs off the framework established here: enduring pattern → functional impairment → dominant interpersonal strategy → boundaries and psychotherapy.*

## The diagnostic gate

FA's **MEDIC** frame: maladaptive, enduring, deviant from cultural norms, inflexible, and causing impairment. Onset is evident by early adulthood and the pattern spans contexts rather than appearing only during an episode.

*Sources: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry pp. 402–403; FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-3, pp. 779–781.*

## The cluster map

| Cluster | Organizing phenotype | Disorders |
|---|---|---|
| A — “weird” | Odd, suspicious, detached | Paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal |
| B — “wild” | Dramatic, emotional, erratic | Borderline, histrionic, narcissistic, antisocial |
| C — “worried” | Anxious, fearful, control/dependence/avoidance | Avoidant, dependent, OCPD |

## Clinical management relationship

The diagnosis predicts the therapeutic interaction. Suspicion can impair alliance, Cluster B instability can split teams, and Cluster C control or dependence can create power struggles. Clear, consistent boundaries are therefore not merely etiquette; they are part of treatment.
