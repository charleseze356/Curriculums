*This lesson zooms into stressor-linked syndromes from the anxiety map. Place the case by qualifying the stressor, identifying the symptom architecture, and then applying duration.*

## Duration is the first separator

| Syndrome | Placement rule |
|---|---|
| Acute stress disorder | Trauma syndrome lasting 3 days to 1 month |
| PTSD | Trauma exposure plus intrusion, avoidance, negative cognition/mood, and hyperarousal for >1 month |
| Adjustment disorder | Depressed mood, anxiety, and/or conduct symptoms within 3 months of a stressor; resolves within 6 months after it ends |

*Sources: FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-10, pp. 795–796; FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry pp. 396–397.*

## Management follows mechanism

Trauma-focused CBT includes exposure and cognitive processing. SSRIs/SNRIs are first-line medications for PTSD, while alpha-1 antagonists can target nightmares. FA Clinical Algorithms lists supportive therapy for adjustment disorder.

## Dissociation: supplemented source

FA Clinical Algorithms does not cover the COMAT's dissociative-disorder category. First Aid Clinical Pattern Recognition supplements the gap: dissociative identity disorder involves at least two distinct identity states and significant autobiographical/daily/traumatic memory gaps, causing impairment and not explained by substances or medication. Psychotherapy directly addresses traumatic memories, safety, stabilization, identity integration, and symptom reduction.

*Supplement source: First Aid Clinical Pattern Recognition, Psychiatry Ch. 13, dissociative identity disorder case/discussion. Depersonalization/derealization disorder and dissociative amnesia remain incompletely sourced in the available primary materials.*
