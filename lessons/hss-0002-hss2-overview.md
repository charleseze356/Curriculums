*This lesson closes Chapter 1. Lesson 0001 gave you the map — the three sciences, the HSS domains, the linking role of systems thinking. This lesson bridges from that map into the practical territory HSS II covers. Read it as the transition from "what is this?" to "how do I use this?"*

## Why this lesson exists

HSS I taught you the vocabulary and the framework. You know what health systems science is, you know its domains, and you know why it matters. But knowing what patient safety *is* is not the same as knowing how to *do* it. Knowing that cost is a problem is not the same as knowing which costs to target.

HSS II is the practical application layer. The syllabus states this explicitly: *"Health System Science II provides more practical application of the concepts that were covered in part 1 as well as new concepts."*

This lesson is the bridge. It maps the HSS I framework onto the HSS II practical competencies so you can see the through-line before diving into specific topics.

*Source: Rowan-Virtua SOM HSS II Syllabus, Section I — Block Overview/Description*

## The HSS II objectives — mapped to the HSS domains

The syllabus lists 10 objectives. Here they are, organized by which HSS domain they belong to (not in syllabus order — in conceptual order):

| Objective | HSS Domain | Load-bearing? |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Describe the healthcare structure and processes | Core functional — Health care structures and processes | **Yes** — everything else depends on understanding how the system is built |
| 8. Define the habits and tools of a systems thinking professional | Linking — Systems thinking | **Yes** — the cognitive habit that connects all domains |
| 4. Explain value in the context of quality, costs, and satisfaction | Core functional — Value in health care | **Yes** — value is the synthesis concept that ties cost, quality, and patient experience together |
| 10. Describe the drivers of healthcare costs | Core functional — Health care policy and economics | Application — depends on understanding structure (Obj 1) |
| 2. Describe population health and its determinants | Core functional — Population health and SDOH | Application — depends on understanding systems thinking (Obj 8) |
| 3. Describe patient-centered care | Core functional — Value in health care | Application — depends on understanding value (Obj 4) |
| 5. Describe quality improvement models | Foundational — Quality improvement | Application — depends on systems thinking (Obj 8) |
| 6. Define patient safety and its relation to quality | Foundational — Patient safety | Application — depends on QI (Obj 5) |
| 7. Describe the systemic causes of errors | Foundational — Patient safety | Application — depends on systems thinking (Obj 8) |
| 9. Outline the effect of digitizing healthcare | Core functional — Clinical informatics | Application — depends on understanding structure (Obj 1) |

**The dependency chain:** Structure (1) → Systems thinking (8) → Value (4) → everything else. This is why the curriculum is organized in this order, not in syllabus order.

*Source: Rowan-Virtua SOM HSS II Syllabus, Section I.B — Objectives*

## What's new in HSS II vs. HSS I

HSS I gave you the framework. HSS II adds:

1. **Practical application of systems change** — not just "what is quality improvement?" but "how do you actually run a PDSA cycle in a clinic?"
2. **Cost analysis** — not just "cost is a problem" but "which costs are drivers, which are necessary, and how do you reduce waste without harming patients?"
3. **Communication technology in healthcare** — how digital tools (EHRs, telehealth, clinical decision support) change how care is delivered
4. **The dynamic nature of HSS** — systems aren't static; policy changes, payment models evolve, and the physician's role shifts with them

The syllabus goals frame this as aligning medical education with the *dynamics* of the healthcare delivery system — not just understanding the system as it is today, but understanding how it changes and how to adapt within it.

*Source: Rowan-Virtua SOM HSS II Syllabus, Section I.A — Goals*

## How the HSS II intersession is structured

This is a one-week intersession (June 29 – July 3, 2026). The structure is:

- **Monday–Thursday:** Mandatory lectures covering the core domains
- **Monday 3–4 PM:** Two Aquifer High Value Care quizzes (Cases 6 and 12)
- **Friday 1 PM:** Written exam (61 questions, closed book, ExamSoft)

The exam is 80% of the grade. The Aquifer quizzes are 10% each. The exam tests application, not just recall — approximately 5% of questions are "application of principle" style.

**What this means for how to study:** The curriculum that follows is organized for *understanding* (Sung: top-down, dependency order). But the exam tests *application*. The knowledge checks in each lesson are orientation checks — can you place this concept in the map? The exam will ask you to *use* the map to navigate a clinical scenario.

## The through-line: from framework to action

| HSS I (framework) | HSS II (application) |
|---|---|
| What is HSS? | How do we apply it? |
| What are the domains? | How do the domains interact in real systems? |
| What is systems thinking? | How do you use systems thinking to diagnose and fix problems? |
| What is value? | How do you measure and improve value in practice? |
| What is patient safety? | How do you identify systemic causes of errors and prevent them? |

Every lesson that follows in this curriculum takes a concept from the left column and moves it to the right column. If you lose the thread at any point, come back to this table.
