## The key idea

Psychiatric diagnoses often share symptoms. FA makes duration a sorting axis across disorder families.

| Family | Duration thresholds |
|---|---|
| Psychotic disorders | Brief psychotic <1 month; schizophreniform 1–6 months; schizophrenia >6 months |
| Mood disorders | Major depression >2 weeks; hypomania >4 days; mania >7 days; dysthymia/cyclothymia >2 years |
| Anxiety/trauma | Acute stress <1 month; PTSD >1 month; GAD >6 months; adjustment resolves within 6 months of stressor termination |

*Source: FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry Ch. 27-1, Disorders by Symptom Duration; Ch. 27-7 and 27-10.*

---

## The shelf move

When two answer choices sound similar, ask:

1. What symptom cluster is present?
2. How long has it been present?
3. Is there impairment, psychosis, hospitalization, or safety risk?

Example: elevated mood with decreased sleep can be mania or hypomania. The difference is not just intensity; it is **duration plus functional consequence**.

---

## Why this is a schema, not trivia

The duration axis lets you compress many labels into one visual timeline. That matters because the exam does not ask, "Define schizophreniform." It gives a patient and asks you to decide what the timeline implies.

If the label changes, management may change too: hospitalization, medication urgency, level of monitoring, and safety planning all depend on where the patient sits on the timeline.
