*This lesson zooms into cue structure from the anxiety map. Place each presentation by asking whether fear is diffuse, an unexpected attack, social scrutiny, a specific object, or inability to escape.*

## Four patterns that look alike

| Pattern | Diagnostic anchor | First-line direction |
|---|---|---|
| GAD | ≥6 months of uncontrollable, multi-domain worry plus associated restlessness, fatigue, concentration difficulty, irritability, muscle tension, or sleep disturbance | CBT + SSRI/SNRI |
| Panic disorder | Recurrent unexpected attacks; ≥1 month of anticipatory concern or avoidance | CBT with graded exposure + SSRI/SNRI |
| Social anxiety | ≥6 months of fear of scrutiny/humiliation in social or performance situations | CBT + SSRI/SNRI; propranolol for performance-only subtype |
| Specific phobia | ≥6 months of disproportionate fear/avoidance of a specific object or situation | Exposure-based CBT |

*Sources: FA Clinical Algorithms, Ch. 27-10, pp. 795–796; FA Step 2 CK 8E, Psychiatry pp. 394–396.*

## The clinical move

Do not diagnose by the word “anxiety.” Diagnose by **what predicts the anxiety**. Diffuse prediction failure suggests GAD; abrupt unexpected attacks suggest panic; social evaluation suggests social anxiety; a stable external cue suggests specific phobia.

## Panic is also a medical differential

Chest pain, palpitations, dyspnea, dizziness, paresthesias, and fear can mimic cardiopulmonary or endocrine disease. The psychiatry label comes after clinically appropriate exclusion of dangerous causes.
