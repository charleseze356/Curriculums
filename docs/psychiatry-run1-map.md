## COMAT Dimension 2 task overlay

| Physician task | Blueprint weight | How curriculum handles it |
|---|---:|---|
| Health promotion / disease prevention / health care delivery | 8–20% | Integrated in management, safety, social determinants, and holistic-treatment checks |
| History and physical, including diagnosis | 30–45% | Every lesson opens from clinical presentation → differential → diagnosis |
| Diagnostic technologies | 5–6% | Placed where relevant: labs/tox, MSE/cognitive testing, polysomnography, rating scales |
| Management | 30–45% | Knowledge checks emphasize next-best-step, level of care, medication/nonpharm choices |
| Scientific mechanisms of disease | 8–10% | Mechanisms taught only when they change diagnosis or management |

## Selected COMAT learner objectives mapped

1. Holistic care including physical, mental, emotional, spiritual well-being and SDOH → integrated across formulation/safety/treatment lessons.
2. Diagnose/treat psychiatric disorders; suicide/homicidal ideation risk; psychiatric evaluation, MSE, risk assessment → Week 1 frame + safety; structured-assessment appendix planned.
3. Diagnose/manage inpatient and outpatient conditions → disposition logic throughout.
4. Multifaceted treatment plans → management-focused checks in every lesson.
5. Psychotropics and nonpharmacologic treatments including psychotherapy, ECT, TMS → mood/psychosis/anxiety/substance chapters.
6. Psychiatric emergencies including acute crises and withdrawal → suicide, psychosis, NMS/serotonin syndrome, intoxication/withdrawal.
